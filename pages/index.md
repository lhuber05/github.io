**Apocalypse Matters** is a digital collection of resources for teaching about the Book of Revelation, aka John's Apocalypse, the final book of the Christian Bible.

Revelation was written during the last quarter of the first century C.E. to communities of Jesus followers living in the urban centers of Asia Minor (modern Türkiye). The author, John, describes writing from an island, called Patmos, which just of the coast of Asia Minor. As an "apocalypse" Revelation imagines the heavenly world and the earthly realm from a heavenly perspective. This makes it seem fantastic and otherworldly. However, the book is clearly embedded within the material world of the eastern Roman Empire. Many of the images in this collection help illuminate this complex world.

Given its place in the Christian canon, Revelation has historically been read as a bridge from the Bible to the present and future. Readers across the centuries have used the book to interpret events, environments, and people around them. Some items in this collection represent these interpretive trajectories.
